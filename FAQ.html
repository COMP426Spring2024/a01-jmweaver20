FAQ

Question Answer
Where is Arachne from? In Greek mythology, Arachne is a Lydian woman born to Idmon, a famous dyer in Lydia.
What is Arachne credited for? Arachne is credited for the invention of linen cloth and nets, while her sister introduced the spindle in the manufactoring of wool.
Why did Athena turn Arachne into a spider? Athena, furious at Arachne's huberis and skill made her a spider to mock her weaving ability.
Why did Athena come to challenge Arachne? Arachne boasted that she was the best weaver that ever existed, even claiming that she was better than the goddess Athena.
What does Arachne's name mean? Arachne means spider in Greek. A rather fitting name for an extremely talented weaver who was then turned into a spider by the goddess Athena.
